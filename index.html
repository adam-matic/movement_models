A single muscle: sensors and a speed controller

One Hill-type muscle with a compliant tendon, anchored at both ends for isometric contraction. Add a spindle and a Golgi tendon organ, then close a muscle-fibre speed control loop with a slider reference — and watch the fibre shorten against its own tendon while the unit length never changes.

A model-free reaching arm

A two-joint, six-muscle arm reaches to targets you set — with no inverse kinematics and no inverse dynamics. A small hierarchy of feedback loops, in the spirit of Perceptual Control Theory, turns position errors into muscle activations. Tune the impedance gains, the spinal force distribution, and the sensory delays, and watch the reach stay stable or break into a ring.

Hill-type muscles

The Hill muscle model captures how muscle force depends on length, velocity, and activation. Explore the force-length and force-velocity curves interactively, and watch how activation dynamics shape the muscle's response to neural input.
