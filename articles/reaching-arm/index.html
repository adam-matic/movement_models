A model-free reaching arm

This is a two-joint, six-muscle arm — the same CompliantTendonArm26 effector used across these articles — reaching to targets without any inverse kinematics or inverse dynamics. There is no trajectory planner and no internal model of the arm's dynamics. Instead, the arm is steered by a small hierarchy of negative-feedback loops in the spirit of Perceptual Control Theory: each level perceives a quantity, compares it to a reference, and acts to cancel the difference. Movement is a side effect of controlling perceptions, not a plan that is executed.

How it works

The controller is a three-level cascade. Nothing in it inverts the arm's geometry or dynamics online — the only body knowledge it carries is a fixed 6×2 matrix mapping task-space errors to muscles.

  • L3 — task position. The hand's position is perceived in polar task coordinates: reach r (distance from the shoulder) and pointing angle φ, both computed by forward kinematics from the sensed joint angles. Two virtual springs-and-dampers turn the position error into a task force command: Qr* = KP,r(r*−r) − KD,rṙ and likewise for φ.
  • L2 — task force. The six Golgi-tendon-organ force sensors are read as a perceived task force, compared with the L3 command, and the error is distributed to the muscles through a fixed weight matrix W. An activation leak bleeds the integrator toward rest.
  • L1 — muscle activation integrates the drive and excites the six Hill-type muscles on the compliant-tendon arm.

The arm is free — it swings under the muscles rather than being clamped. That is essential: the motion itself gives the fast inner force loop the velocity damping it otherwise lacks, so the reach is stable where an isometric force loop would ring. Targets are approached through a reference governor that ramps the commanded reference to the target at a bounded rate; a literal step overshoots.

Things to try

  • Drive it to instability. Push KP,r up while lowering KD,r — the reach axis starts to ring. Damping and stiffness trade off exactly as in a physical spring.
  • Feel the delays. Raise the GTO (Ib) delay toward 40–60 ms. The inner force loop loses phase margin and the settling ring grows — this is the "delays set the ceiling" result from the underlying research: transport latency, not gain, is what limits how hard the arm can be driven.
  • Stiffen without moving. Raise the co-contraction reference c*. Total tendon force climbs and the arm gets stiffer against the target while the hand barely moves — stiffness is an independent controlled variable.
  • Reach across the workspace by clicking far targets. The single fixed gain set holds sub-centimetre accuracy over the whole reachable annulus.

The controller here is a faithful port of the current best reaching controller from the musclesim research project (arm2dof/gto_reaching_2dof.py), with its deployed gains and its evolution-tuned distribution matrix. The physics runs entirely in your browser on motornet.js, a JavaScript port of MotorNet. Everything above is a live simulation, not a recording.
